Common dog choices

Which one is your dog?


Siberian sled dog

Siberian sled dog is a primitive and ancient breed, mainly living in northeast Siberia and southern Greenland. Husky's name comes from his unique hoarseness.

Husky's personality is changeable, some are extremely timid, and some are extremely violent. Husky, who has entered the human society and family, has no such extreme personality and is more docile. He is a kind of pet dog popular all over the world.

Golden Retriever

The swimming endurance of the retriever dog is excellent.

Golden Retriever is one of the most common domestic dogs. It is easy to raise, patient and requires little from its owner. It only needs regular exercise, feeding and veterinary examination.

It belongs to a well proportioned, powerful and lively breed. It is characterized by stability, reasonable coordination of all parts of the body, neither too long nor clumsy legs, friendly expression, warm personality, alertness, self-confidence, and not afraid of being unripe.

Golden Retriever is a kind of retriever, mostly used as guide dog and pet dog.

Labrador

Labrador, also known as retriever, is a medium and large dog. It is very suitable to be selected as a guide dog or subway police dog, search and rescue dog and other working dog in public places. It is named for its origin in Newfoundland and Labrador in Canada.

Labrador retriever has four colors: black, yellow, chocolate and off white. The most common are black and yellow. Labrador is the most registered breed in the American Kennel Club. It is especially friendly to children and a little clingy to dog owners.

Springer

Spenger is a medium-sized dog with a strong body.

It is characterized by a pair of long drooping ears.

With its sensitive sense of smell and excellent search ability, Springer dog plays an important role in the field of drug search, explosive search and blood search.

Compared with other breeds of dogs, spenger has the characteristics of high excitability, strong desire for possession and good search endurance, but also has the disadvantages of small courage and weak adaptability to the environment.

Pointers

It originated in England in the 17th century.

The first reliable record of indicator hounds in England appeared in 1650.

The behavioral characteristics of all kinds of indicator hounds are that when they find prey, they will use a specific posture to indicate where the prey is.

In the early days, the indicator hound was an assistant for hunters to use nets to catch birds, especially birds of the kyolin. In the 18th century, hunting birds and rabbits with guns became popular. The Spanish indicator hound began to spread to Europe. The dog used its sense of smell to track its prey along the ground.
